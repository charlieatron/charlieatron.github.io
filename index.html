About Hand Differences

Hand differences are a birth defect that change the shape of someone's hand. It could be a lack of a few fingers, the fusing of two or more fingers, or something entirely different. This anomalies can take the form of many diagnoses, such as syndactyly or polydactyly. Most of the time, people are born with this condition and live with it their entire lives, and it is the only thing they ever know. However, when they meet someone with a "normal" hand, those people don't know how to react. What people need to know is that no question they could ask someone with this condition, within obvious reason, is "over the line" in any sort of way. It's perfectly okay to make some assumptions, as long as you talk about them in a polite way. I know this from experience, since I was born with only two fingers on my right hand. The best encounters I've had when meeting new people consist of them asking "Hey, were you born with that?" and then forgetting about it. This kind of encounter easily and cleanly eliminates the elephant in the room surrounding one's hand, allowing everyone involved to get on with their day. Hopefully by reading this you now know how to react to meeting someone with this type of birth defect.
